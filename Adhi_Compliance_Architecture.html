1. Executive Summary

Adhi Compliance is an AI-powered compliance management platform developed by RootedAI that enables organizations deploying artificial intelligence to navigate the rapidly evolving global regulatory landscape with confidence.

As AI regulation intensifies — the EU AI Act mandating fines up to €35 million, over 700 AI-related bills across 45 US states, and emerging frameworks in India and China — organizations face an unprecedented compliance burden with virtually zero purpose-built infrastructure.

Adhi Compliance provides:

  • Automated Risk Classification — auto-classify AI systems per EU AI Act risk tiers (Unacceptable, High, Limited, Minimal) and other jurisdictional frameworks
  • Continuous Compliance Monitoring — real-time tracking across EU, US, India, and China regulatory regimes with proactive alerting
  • Bias & Fairness Auditing — automated bias detection in AI model outputs using statistical and ML methods
  • Transparency Documentation — automated model cards, data sheets, and impact assessments
  • Incident Reporting — streamlined EU AI Act Article 62 workflows with deadline tracking
  • Cross-Jurisdiction Mapping — unified compliance view across multiple regulatory regimes
Heritage: Adhi Compliance evolved from a multimodal RAG system capable of ingesting video, audio, PDF, and image data for semantic search. This ingestion pipeline is retained as foundation for processing complex regulatory documents and audit artifacts.

Architectural Principles

  • API-First — all functionality exposed via versioned REST endpoints; the frontend is a first-party consumer
  • Event-Driven — long-running tasks handled asynchronously via Celery + Redis
  • Multi-Tenant Isolation — Row-Level Security at database level for complete data isolation
  • AI-Native — LLM and embedding models are core infrastructure, not bolt-ons
  • Regulation-as-Data — frameworks are structured, versioned, queryable datasets

6.3 Bias Detection & Embedding Pipeline

Bias Auditor implements four fairness metrics:

  • Demographic Parity — P(Ŷ=1|A=0) = P(Ŷ=1|A=1); equal positive rates across groups
  • Equalized Odds — equal TPR and FPR across groups
  • Disparate Impact Ratio — selection rate ratio (<0.8 = potential disparate impact)
  • Calibration — predicted probabilities match actuals per group

Statistical significance via Chi-squared / Fisher's exact tests. LLM interprets metrics into recommendations.

7. Compliance Engine Architecture

7.1 Regulation Loader & Auto-Classification

The regulation loader ingests from multiple sources:

  • Official Gazettes — EU Official Journal, India Gazette, US Federal Register
  • Legislative Databases — EUR-Lex, congress.gov, PRS India
  • Curated Packages — manually verified regulation bundles

Pipeline: text extraction → structural parsing → requirement extraction → embedding → cross-reference linking.

Auto-classification on new AI system registration:

  1. Keyword matching against prohibited use patterns (Art. 5)
  2. Category matching against Annex III high-risk list
  3. LLM analysis for ambiguous cases (structured JSON output)
  4. Confidence scoring — low-confidence flagged for human review
  5. Multi-jurisdiction mapping for all applicable regions

Incident Reporting Workflow (EU AI Act Article 62)

  1. Detection — user report or automated anomaly trigger
  2. Severity Assessment — LLM-assisted triage: "serious incident" per Art. 62?
  3. Data Collection — guided form: system ID, description, affected parties, timeline
  4. Authority Mapping — identifies relevant authority (AI Office for EU, MEITY for India)
  5. Report Generation — structured report in required format
  6. Deadline Tracking — 15-day submission deadline with automated reminders
  7. Follow-up — manages required follow-up reports and resolution
